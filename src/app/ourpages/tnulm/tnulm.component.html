TNULM

Tamilnadu - Urban Livelihood Mission (TNULM) intends to reduce poverty and vulnerability of the urban poor households by enabling them to access gainful self-employment and skilled wage employment opportunities, resulting in an appreciable improvement in their livelihoods on a sustainable basis, through building strong grassroots level institutions of the poor. The scheme is being implemented from the year 2014-15. TNULM is a centrally sponsored scheme funded by both the Central and the State Governments in the ratio of 60:40. 

The components of the TNULM are:

1.   Social Mobilization and Institution Development (SM&ID).
2.   Capacity Building and Training (CB&T).
3.   Employment through Skill Training and Placement (EST&P).
4.   Self Employment Programme (SEP).
5.   Support to Urban Street Vendors (SUH).
6.   Scheme of Shelter for Urban Homeless (SUSV), and an exclusive component, Innovation & Special Projects (I&SP)
